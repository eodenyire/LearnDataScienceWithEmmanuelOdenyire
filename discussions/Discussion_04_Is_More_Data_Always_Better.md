# Discussion 04: Is More Data Always Better?

## The Debate
Data scientists often say "more data beats better algorithms." But is this always true? Consider:
- **Garbage in, garbage out**: More bad data can hurt models
- **Data quality vs. quantity**: A small clean dataset may outperform a large noisy one
- **Computational cost**: More data requires more resources
- **Privacy concerns**: More data = more privacy risk

## Discussion Prompts

### Part A: Take a Position (200+ words)
Argue **for or against** the statement: *"In the era of big data, quantity of data is more important than quality."*

Support your argument with:
- At least one real-world example or research finding
- Consideration of the counterargument
- Conditions under which your position might not hold

### Part B: Practical Scenarios
For each scenario, decide whether you'd prioritize **more data** or **better quality data**, and explain why:
1. Training a medical diagnosis model for rare diseases
2. Training a large language model for general text generation
3. Building a fraud detection model for a bank
4. Predicting crop yields for smallholder farmers in rural areas

### Part C: Data Quality Framework (100+ words)
Propose a **data quality scorecard** with 5–7 dimensions (e.g., completeness, consistency, accuracy). How would you use this to decide whether to collect more data or clean existing data?
