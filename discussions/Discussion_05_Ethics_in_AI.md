# Discussion 05: Ethics in AI

## Why This Matters
AI systems are increasingly making or influencing decisions that affect people's lives: loan approvals, medical diagnoses, hiring, criminal sentencing, and more. As data scientists, we have ethical responsibilities.

## Core Ethical Principles in AI
1. **Fairness**: Does the model treat all groups equitably?
2. **Transparency**: Can the model's decisions be explained?
3. **Accountability**: Who is responsible when AI causes harm?
4. **Privacy**: Is personal data being used responsibly?
5. **Beneficence**: Does the AI genuinely help people?

## Discussion Prompts

### Part A: Ethical Dilemma (200+ words)
Choose one of these scenarios and analyze the ethical implications:
1. A bank uses an AI model that approves loans at higher rates for one demographic. The model is highly accurate overall but discriminatory.
2. A government uses AI to predict which citizens are likely to commit crimes (predictive policing).
3. A social media platform uses AI to maximize engagement, even if it surfaces divisive content.

Discuss: What is the harm? Who is responsible? What would you do as the data scientist who built it?

### Part B: AI in Africa (150+ words)
What unique ethical considerations exist when deploying AI systems in African countries? Consider:
- Data collection on historically marginalized populations
- Models trained on Western data applied in African contexts
- Limited regulatory frameworks

### Part C: The Data Scientist's Role
What responsibility does a data scientist have when they discover their model is causing harm? Do you have an obligation to speak up even if your employer disagrees? (100+ words)
